Dohyun Kim

Dohyun Kim received the Ph.D. degree in Computational Science and Engineering from Yonsei University, Seoul, Republic of Korea, in 2021. His thesis advisor was Professor Eun-Jae Park.

He is particularly interested in discontinuous Galerkin (DG) and staggered discontinuous Galerkin (SDG) methods for elliptic equations and related mathematical analysis together with simulations.
Currently, He is working on polygonal SDG (polySDG) methods for fluid dynamics including the Navier-Stokes problem, the Boussinesq problem, and the Darcy flow in porous media on polygonal meshes.

PolySDG

Polygonal staggered discontinuous Galerkin (polySDG) is a finite element method:

  • Works on polygonal meshes
  • Robust to mesh distortion
  • Locally conservative
  • Stable without stabilizing terms

Learn More

Mesh (MATLAB class)

Mesh is a MATLAB class for finite element methods:

  • Easy to use
  • Works for 1D, 2D, 3D simplices
  • Automatic facet generation from given mesh for interior/boundary
  • Automatic generation of affine information of given mesh
